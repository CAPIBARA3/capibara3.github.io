\section{Membership Tiers \& Benefits}

\subsection{Tier 1: Student Member}
\paragraph{Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
\item Currently enrolled student (high school undergraduate, graduate)
\item Signed Membership agreement
\item Complete onboarding checklist (\texttt{env} guides only), including joining GitHub organisation
\item Minimum of 2 hours/week contribution
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Benefits:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Write Access to all team's repositories
\item Listed as collaboration member on website (with consent)
\item Eligible for authorship per AUTHORSHIP\_POLICY.md
\item May represent CAPIBARA at conferences
\item Vote in decisions
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Tier 2: External Contributor}
\paragraph{Requirements:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Contributes once or occasionally
\item May be professionals, alumni, or irregular students
\item Code of Conduct agreement (by contributing to a repository you agree to it)
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Benefits:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Access to relevant GitHub repositories
\item Fairly regular contributors may be listed on the website as such
\item May earn authorship for substantial contributions, regulated by AUTHORSHIP\_POLICY.md
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Tier 3: Advisory Member}
\paragraph{Requirements:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Faculty, industry professional, or senior scientist
\item Agrees to mentor/advise 1 hour/month
\item Approved by Programme Leads
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Benefits:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Listed on website as member of the Advisory Board
\item Access to the GitHub repositories
\item Invitation to non-advising meetings
\end{itemize}


\section{Requirements}

Additionally, there are general requirements to ensure seamless and enriching collaboration:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Strong English communication (oral and written) is required for collaboration and outreach
\item Curiosity and interest in a field(s), not necessarily experience or expertise in that field
    \begin{enumerate}
    \item For technical and scientific positions: in mathematics, physical sciences, chemistry or engineering
    \item For outreach and adminstration positions: in communication, arts, education or law
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

The following skills are not required at all, but will ease your onboarding process and collaboration: strong academic record, experience in a specific field, GitHub and Python knowledge. Students that only partially fulfill the requirements outlined in this section may also be accepted to be part of CAPIBARA upon approval of programme lead.

\section{Application Process}

\subsection{For Student Members:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Submit application via website form
\item Interview with Programme Lead (30 min video call) (optional)
\item Receive email requesting signed documents as PDF
\item Complete onboarding tutorials to get started
\item 30-day probationary period
\item Full membership granted upon Programme Lead approval
\end{enumerate}

Prospective students can visit a step by step guide in \href{../onboarding/00_applying_to_join.md}{here}.

\subsection{For Advisory Members:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Invitation from Programme Lead or contact us
\item Review of CV/background
\item Meeting with Programme Lead
\item Signed Memorandum of Understanding
\end{enumerate}

\section{Expectations \& Responsibilities}

\subsection{All Members Must:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Contribute constructively to team goals
\item Communicate availability changes
\item Document their work
\item Respect collaboration policies
\item Attend meetings
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Time Commitment:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Minimum:} 2 hours/week for Student Members
\item \textbf{Recommended:} 3+ hours/week for leadership roles
\item \textbf{Tracking:} Via GitHub commit log
\end{itemize}

\section{Removal of Membership}

\subsection{Grounds for Removal:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Inactivity:} No contributions for 12 months without notice
\item \textbf{Violations:} Repeated breaches of Code of Conduct or any of the Collaboration's policies
\item \textbf{Harmful Actions:} Deliberate sabotage, harassment, data theft
\item \textbf{Misrepresentation:} Falsifying credentials or contributions
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Removal Process (Progressive):}
\begin{verbatim}
Incident → Private Warning → 30-Day Probation → Vote → Removal
\end{verbatim}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Documentation:} Incident report by Programmeh Lead
\item \textbf{Private Discussion:} With member to understand situation
\item \textbf{Probation Period:} 30 days to improve (except for severe violations)
\item \textbf{Team Vote:} Majority vote of Programme Lead + CoC committee
\item \textbf{Notification:} Formal email with reasons and appeal process
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Appeal Process:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Written appeal within 14 days of removal
\item Reviewed by 3-member panel (1 Advisory, 2 Student Members not involved)
\item Decision within 30 days
\item Final decision binding
\end{enumerate}

\section{Alumni Status}
\begin{itemize}
\item Graduating students may become Associate Members
\item Maintain website listing (with ``Alumni'' tag)
\item Invited to some events
\item May continue contributing if desired
\item Removed members may not be included in the alumni community
\end{itemize}

\section{Institutional Membership}
For universities/labs wishing to formally partner:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Signed Memorandum of Understanding (MoU template coming)
\item Designated faculty liaison
\item Resource contribution (lab space, compute, etc.)
\end{enumerate}
