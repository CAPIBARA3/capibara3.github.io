CAPIBARA COLLABORATION GOVERNANCE CHARTER (Draft v0.2)#

Last Updated: November 23, 2025 Status: Draft Applies to: All members, contributors, and external collaborators

1. Name & Status#

The CAPIBARA Collaboration (“the Collaboration”) is a volunteer-based international student research collective focused on space science and high-energy astrophysics instrumentation.

The Collaboration is not presently incorporated as a legal entity. All activities are conducted on a best-effort, non-commercial, educational basis.

2. Mission#

  • To design, develop, test, and deploy cosmic-ray (short term) and high-energy transient (long term) detectors

  • To support student development in space science, data analysis, and engineering

  • To produce openly licensed scientific knowledge whenever possible

  • To establish a student-led CubeSat constellation to study transient high-energy phenomena

3. Membership#

3.1 Eligibility#

Membership is open to:

  • High school, undergraduate, or graduate students

  • Individuals with relevant technical skills or scientific interests

  • Members must agree to the Membership Agreement and Code of Conduct

No institutional affiliation is required.

3.2 Minors#

Members under the age of 18 require parental or guardian authorization.

3.3 Joining#

Applicants must:

  • Submit an application

  • Agree to all Collaboration policies

  • Be approved by the Project Lead

3.4 Leaving#

Members may leave voluntarily at any time. Contributions already merged remain part of the project under the applicable license.

Repeated violation of the Code of Conduct or policies may result in removal.

4. Governance#

4.1 Core Roles#

  • Project Lead / Principal Investigator (PI) – currently held by the founder (@joanalnu)

  • Technical Roles - for each mission and each scientific project

4.2 Leadership Selection#

  • The founding PI holds interim authority

  • Leadership structure may be modified by majority vote of active contributors

  • Elections or rotating leadership may be introduced as membership grows

4.3 Decision Making#

  • Technical decisions occur via GitHub issues, discussions, or pull requests

  • Major strategic decisions are made by consensus or majority voting

  • Members may propose changes to this charter via pull request

5. Collaboration Structure#

The Collaboration operates through:

  • GitHub (primary workspace)

  • Asynchronous written communication

  • Public and private repositories as required

  • Optional WhatsApp for informal contact only

6. Policies & Documents#

The following documents govern participation and must be agreed to:

  • Code of Conduct

  • Membership Agreement

  • Authorship & Publication Policy

  • IP & Licensing Policy

  • Data & Communications Policy

All policies are versioned, public, and amendable via GitHub pull request.

7. Intellectual Property & Licensing Principles#

By default:

  • Code is released under BSD-3-Clause

  • Documentation under CC-BY 4.0 or CC-BY-SA 4.0

  • Members retain copyright to their contributions

  • Members grant the Collaboration a perpetual, nonexclusive license to use contributions

  • Private development for safety/security is allowed temporarily

Full details are in the IP Policy (separate document).

8. Publications & Attribution#

All scientific publications must:

  • Acknowledge the CAPIBARA Collaboration

  • Follow the Authorship & Publication Policy

  • Use open access whenever possible

9. External Relationships & Representation#

Only designated representatives may speak on behalf of the Collaboration in formal communications. Members must not claim endorsement by the Collaboration without authorization.

10. Conflict Resolution#

Conflicts should be resolved informally if possible. If needed:

  1. Peer mediation

  2. Review by Project Lead

  3. Review by a designated conflict resolution committee (future)

  4. Removal in severe cases

Harassment or discrimination will not be tolerated (see Code of Conduct).

11. Amendments#

This charter may be amended by:

  • Majority vote of active members

  • OR by founder decision during early phase until membership exceeds N=25

Amendments must be documented in GitHub.

12. Dissolution#

If the Collaboration disbands:

  • Existing open-source code remains open

  • Research data will remain publicly archived whenever possible

  • Project name and branding will be retired or transferred to a stewarding institution

13. Transparency#

Except where safety, legal, or privacy concerns apply:

  • Documents and code will be public

  • Decision-making will be logged on GitHub

  • Meeting notes will be archived when possible
