# CAPIBARA Collaboration Membership Agreement (Draft v0.2)

**Last Updated:** November 23, 2025
**Status:** Draft
**Applies to:** All members, contributors, and external collaborators

---

## 1. Purpose of Agreement

This agreement defines the rights and responsibilities of individuals (“Members”) participating in the **CAPIBARA Collaboration** (“the Collaboration”).

It is intended to:

* Set expectations for participation
* Protect member contributions under clear license terms
* Establish behavior and communication standards
* Enable safe collaboration with minors
* Clarify ownership of code, data, and publications

By joining, Members voluntarily agree to the terms below.

---

## 2. Nature of Participation

* The Collaboration is a **volunteer, educational scientific project**.
* No employment relationship is created.
* Members are not required to pay fees.
* Participation is on a best-effort and goodwill basis.

---

## 3. Eligibility

Members must:

* Agree to this Membership Agreement and Code of Conduct
* Provide accurate name and contact information
* Be at least 13 years old
* Obtain parental/guardian consent if under 18

The Collaboration reserves the right to decline or remove any member.

---

## 4. Conduct & Community Standards

Members must follow:

* The CAPIBARA **Code of Conduct**
* Local laws and regulations
* Ethical scientific behavior

Harassment, discrimination, and harmful conduct are grounds for removal.

---

## 5. Intellectual Property (IP) & Licensing

### 5.1 Ownership

Members **retain copyright** to any code, writing, designs, or data they independently create.

### 5.2 License to the Collaboration

By contributing, Members grant the Collaboration a:

* Nonexclusive
* Worldwide
* Free
* Irrevocable
* Perpetual license

To use, modify, reproduce, and distribute the contribution under the project’s open-source license(s).

### 5.3 Changes to Licensing

Licensing changes require:

* Discussion and review
* Approval by project leadership
* Notification to contributors

---

## 6. Confidentiality & Private Information

Some discussions, repositories, or documents may be private. Members agree:

* Not to publicly distribute private internal information
* Not to leak unfinished designs, personal contact info, or security information

Public data and repositories remain free to use under their licenses.

---

## 7. Attribution, Publications & Credit

Members who contribute substantially to scientific results or code are entitled to:

* Authorship or acknowledgment in publications
* Proper credit in public releases

The **Publication & Authorship Policy** defines the precise rules.

---

## 8. Leaving the Collaboration

Members can leave at any time. Contributions already made remain under the project license.

Leadership may remove members for:

* Violating the Code of Conduct
* Legal or ethical misconduct
* Security or safety concerns
* Extended inactivity if necessary for operations

When possible, removals will include a warning and review process.

---

## 9. Minors & Parental Consent

For members under 18:

* A parent/guardian must review and agree to this membership
* Parent/guardian acknowledges that participation is voluntary and educational
* Collaboration communication will occur in group settings whenever possible
* No member is permitted to request personal or private contact with minors

---

## 10. Liability & Responsibility

The Collaboration:

* Is not a legal entity (at present)
* Provides no insurance, salary, or warranty
* Is not responsible for personal losses, injuries, or legal issues

Members participate at their own risk.

---

## 11. Amendments

This agreement may be updated. Members will be notified via GitHub or email. Continued participation implies agreement to updated terms.

---

## 12. Agreement

By signing or submitting the membership form (or equivalent digital acknowledgment), the Member affirms that:

1. They have read and understood this Membership Agreement.
2. They agree to comply with Collaboration rules and policies.
3. If under 18, a parent or legal guardian has given permission.
