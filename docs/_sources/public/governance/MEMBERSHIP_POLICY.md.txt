# CAPIBARA Collaboration Membership Policy
## Version 1.0 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026

### 1. Membership Tiers & Benefits

#### Tier 1: Student Member (Core Contributor)
**Requirements:**
- Currently enrolled student (high school, undergraduate, graduate)
- Minimum 3 hours/week contribution
- Complete onboarding checklist (`env` guides only)
- Signed Membership agreement

**Benefits:**
- Access to GitHub repositories
- Listed as collaboration member on website (with consent)
- Eligible for authorship per AUTHORSHIP_POLICY.md
- May represent CAPIBARA at conferences
- Vote in technical decisions

#### Tier 2: Associate Member (Occasional Contributor)
**Requirements:**
- Contributes <3 hours/week
- May be professionals, alumni, or irregular students
- Signed Code of Conduct agreement

**Benefits:**
- Access to relevant GitHub repositories
- Listed as associate member
- May earn authorship for substantial contributions

#### Tier 3: Advisory Member
**Requirements:**
- Faculty, industry professional, or senior scientist
- Agrees to mentor/advise 1 hour/month
- Approved by Program Directors

**Benefits:**
- Listed on website Advisory Board
- Access to all non-sensitive documents
- Invitation to quarterly strategy meetings

### 2. Application Process

#### For Student Members:
1. Submit application via website form
2. Complete introductory tasks (join GitHub organisation and onboarding tutorials)
3. Interview with Program Director (30 min video call)
4. 30-day probationary period
5. Full membership granted upon team lead approval

#### For Advisory Members:
1. Invitation from Program Directors
2. Review of CV/background
3. Meeting with Program Directors
4. Signed Memorandum of Understanding

### 3. Expectations & Responsibilities

#### All Members Must:
- Contribute constructively to team goals
- Communicate availability changes
- Document their work
- Respect collaboration policies
- Attend monthly meetings (or watch recordings)

#### Time Commitment:
- **Minimum:** 3 hours/week for Student Members
- **Recommended:** 3+ hours/week for leadership roles
- **Tracking:** Self-reported via monthly survey

### 4. Removal of Membership

#### Grounds for Removal:
1. **Inactivity:** No contributions for 12 months without notice
2. **Violations:** Repeated breaches of Code of Conduct
3. **Harmful Actions:** Deliberate sabotage, harassment, data theft
4. **Misrepresentation:** Falsifying credentials or contributions

#### Removal Process (Progressive):
```
Incident → Private Warning → 30-Day Probation → Vote → Removal
```
1. **Documentation:** Incident report by Program Director
2. **Private Discussion:** With member to understand situation
3. **Probation Period:** 30 days to improve (except for severe violations)
4. **Team Vote:** Majority vote of Program Directors + Advisory Board
5. **Notification:** Formal email with reasons and appeal process

#### Appeal Process:
1. Written appeal within 14 days of removal
2. Reviewed by 3-member panel (1 Advisory, 2 Student Members not involved)
3. Decision within 30 days
4. Final decision binding

### 5. Alumni Status
- Graduating students may become Associate Members
- Maintain website listing (with "Alumni" tag)
- Invited tosome events
- May continue contributing if desired

### 6. Institutional Membership
For universities/labs wishing to formally partner:
1. Signed Memorandum of Understanding (MOU template coming)
2. Designated faculty liaison
3. Resource contribution (lab space, compute, etc.)
