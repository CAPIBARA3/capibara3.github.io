## Part 3: What Does Participation Involve?

Please read and initial each item to show you understand:

☐ **Online research activities** (_____)  
Your child will participate in scientific research including data analysis, coding, hardware design, or documentation—all online via GitHub and video calls.

☐ **Time commitment** (_____)  
About 2 hours per week on average (flexible—more when they can, less during exams).

☐ **Online platforms** (_____)  
They'll use GitHub (for collaboration), email, and video conferencing (Zoom/Google Meet/etc.).

☐ **Volunteer/unpaid** (_____)  
This is educational—there's no payment, but they gain real research experience.

☐ **Group communication only** (_____)  
Adult members will NOT message your child privately. All communication happens in group settings (GitHub, group chats, team meetings).

☐ **Public recognition** (_____)  
Your child's name and contributions may appear on our website and in scientific publications (with additional consent below).

☐ **Can quit anytime** (_____)  
Your child (or you) can leave at any time, no questions asked.

## Part 6: Understanding the Risks

**This is an online activity with typical internet risks**:
- Exposure to inappropriate content (we'll minimize and moderate)
- Technical frustrations (coding can be hard!)
- Time management challenges (balancing with school)

**This is an informal student group**:
- We're not a registered organization or company
- No formal insurance or legal protections
- Supervision is peer-led (students leading students with some faculty advisors)
- We do our best but can't guarantee perfect oversight

☐ I understand and accept these risks

☐ I confirm my child has appropriate health/liability insurance through family or school
