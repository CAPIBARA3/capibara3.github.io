Joan Alcaide-Núñez

×

I created the CAPIBARA Collaboration in 2024, and now lead the development of the CAPIBARA-COSMOS program. The goal of COSMOS is to gradually launch a microsatellite constellation for all-sky, continuous high-energy transient monitoring.

My research in Multi-Messenger Cosmology focuses on how combining multiple “messengers” (GW and EM sources) will enable us to reduce the uncertainty on cosmological parameters, possibly unveiling new hints about the nature of dark energy, dark matter, and the evolution of the Universe. I am deeply interested in how these high-z probes can help us test theoretical models, both accepted and more exotic ones.

Previous work includes analysis of Cepheid (2023) and SNIa (2024) distances to learn about the expansion of the universe. I’ve demonstrated research excellence through the Youth and Science program by Fundació Catalunya La Pedrera, a research stay at ICE-CSIC-IEEC and at OAB-INAF. I also conducted an internship at DLR, where I used Python to analyze thermal images. All this while at the German School of Barcelona, where I obtained a 1.0 Abitur (equivalent to 4.0/4.0 GPA).

Committed to outreach, I’ve participated in the CosmoCaixa Explainers program, tutored STEM subjects weekly, and mentored aspiring STEM students in my community.

In a couple of worlds, I thrive to learn about our place in the cosmos guided by my curiosity and passion for science, technology, and community.

Explore further: my CV here, CAPIBARA-COSMOS, multi-messenger research.
