Supernova Remnant Environments

Supernova Remnants (SNRs) are the expanding materials left after a supernova explosion. Supernovae are classified as either Core Collapse (massive stars) or Thermonuclear (white dwarf explosions), each producing different types of stellar remnants. SNRs allow us to study chemical element formation and contribute heavy metals to new stars. Their X-ray emissions also reveal the possible presence of neutron stars. We aim to identify particles in the core of SNRs by studying their influence on shock waves.
