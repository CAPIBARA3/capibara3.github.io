New Gamma-Ray Detector Prototype Successfully Tested

Our team has successfully tested a new prototype for the gamma-ray detector payload with improved sensitivity.

The new gamma-ray detector prototype during testing (Credit: CAPIBARA Team)

The CAPIBARA team is excited to announce a major milestone in our gamma-ray detector development. After months of research and development, we've successfully tested a new prototype that shows significant improvements in sensitivity and energy resolution.

Technical Improvements

Our new design incorporates several key innovations:

  • Enhanced silicon photomultipliers (SiPMs) with 35% better photon detection efficiency
  • New tungsten collimator design reduces background noise by 27%
  • Improved onboard processing reduces data transmission requirements

Schematic of the new detector design

These improvements will allow us to study fainter gamma-ray sources with greater precision, potentially revealing new details about high-energy astrophysical phenomena.

Next Steps

With the prototype validated, our engineering team is now working on:

  1. Space qualification testing
  2. Integration with the satellite bus
  3. Development of calibration procedures

"This breakthrough in detector technology will significantly enhance our ability to study gamma-ray bursts and active galactic nuclei. The improved sensitivity could help us detect previously unseen phenomena in the high-energy universe."
